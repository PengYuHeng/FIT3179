Global Human Trafficking

From the Year 2005 to 2018, a total of 47167 cases of human trafficking has been reported in Countries participating in the creation of the original dataset. This visualisation was created to raise awareness of the number of cases of human trafficking around the world, keep in mind that these numbers only cover the number reported, there are still many cases. This visualisation was inspired by the movie "No More Bets" 2023

Cases By Age Group and Gender

As you can see, females tend to be trafficked more than males. From at least 2 times to up to 20 times more females trafficked than males. The age group most trafficked is the 9-17 age group, closely followed by the groups from 20 to 38. Whereas the 0-8 and 48+ groups are the least trafficked.

Change in Number of Cases

The number of cases have been rapid increasing from 2014, with the cases almost doubling each year. However from 2016 onwards there has been a sharp decline in the number of cases. This could be due to either the traffickers getting better at hiding their crimes, or the police getting better at catching them.

Top 10 Countries with the most cases from 2005 - 2018

The top countries are mostly from Eastern Europe and South East Asia, with poorer countries at the top. This information indicates that economic factors are in play when it comes to human trafficking.
